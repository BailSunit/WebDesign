Did you know?

 The nicknames ‘bull’ and ‘bear’ sound funny, but they have significant meanings in the foreign exchange market. The bulls are optimistic traders who believe that the foreign exchange prices will rise at a particular time while the bears are those traders who expect the exchange rates to drop in the future.

Did you know that the United Kingdom has a bigger currency exchange market than the United States? It’s no surprise that the USD is the most traded currency in the world, and accepted worldwide as a legal tender. Besides, 87 per cent of all trades involve the USD. Since the USD is the most popular in the international market, most traders perceive that the United States is the center for forex trading, but it’s not. Only 19 per cent of all foreign exchanges occur in the US. The UK is the center of forex trading, with 41 per cent of all the world’s currency exchanges taking place in the country.

An average of 5.3 trillion US dollars gets traded on the foreign exchange market each day. It’s about 53 times higher than the New York Stock Exchange and equivalent to four times the world’s GDP. It is so big that it can buy a car for each American household.
